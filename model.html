Cluster 6: Islands and remote Territories

Countries: Antigua and Barbuda, Aruba, Bahamas, Belize, Bermuda, Bhutan, British Virgin Islands, Brunei, Curacao, East Timor, Eritrea, Fiji, French Polynesia, Greenland, Grenada, Guyana, Kiribati, Kosovo, Liechtenstein, Macao, Maldives, Marshall Islands, Micronesia (country), Nauru, New Caledonia, Palau, Papua New Guinea, Saint Kitts and Nevis, Saint Vincent and the Grenadines, Samoa, Sint Maarten (Dutch part), Solomon Islands, Somalia, South Sudan, Sudan, Suriname, Tonga, Turks and Caicos Islands, Tuvalu, Vanuatu

Key Features: These countries tend to have small populations, limited industrial activity, and are often characterized by geographical vulnerabilities. Island nations like the Maldives, Kiribati, and Tuvalu are particularly susceptible to the impacts of climate change, including rising sea levels and extreme weather events. Many of these nations rely heavily on external trade and aid due to their small economic base. The combination of low emissions and a high dependence on global economic systems often places them in a unique position of needing international support for sustainable development.

Landlocked and Developing Economies

Countries: Bhutan, Nepal, South Sudan, Sudan

Key Features: Landlocked countries like Bhutan and Nepal face challenges related to trade, transportation, and access to resources, which can hinder economic development. South Sudan and Sudan are still recovering from political and conflict-related instability, which affects their economic activity and infrastructure. These nations typically have lower emissions due to limited industrial activity, but they are still vulnerable to climate impacts such as drought, floods, and resource scarcity, especially for their agricultural sectors.
